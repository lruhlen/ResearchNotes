After going over this with a fine-toothed comb, my conclusion is that the differences between Peter's results and mine are due to numerical precision differences caused by the following:

  1. The dMhalf problem I noticed above. Fixed it now. This required slightly modifying my G1J and G4J subroutines.

  2. In calculating G4J, he assumes Pj+1 - Pj is the same as the "right hand side" of eqn 5.39. This is not, in fact, true-- that's why we need to solve those difference equations implicitly in the first place!

  3. He uses a less accurate and less precise value of gravG than I do. It turns out to make a difference.

  4. The resolution of my eos and kappa tables are different, which leads to 4th-place differences in the look-up values between his code and mine.

  5. In spite of the fact that his G values seem to differ from mine by several orders of magnitude, the salient measure of how well either code is working is what the ratio of Gx/x is (i.e., how far off the initial guess difference equations are). By that metric, his results and mine seem reasonable.
