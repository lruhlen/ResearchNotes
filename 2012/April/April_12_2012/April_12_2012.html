  • The conclusions I draw from Figures 1 and 2 are:

    • It doesn't matter whether you start your model out with or without fusion. As long as you can move from the initial input polytropic model to a converged model at the next timestep, you're fine.

    • If you evolve a converged model forward without fusion (but with Crad and Cwrk fully on), the model will *always* evolve down to the same shape (for the luminosity profile, at least).

    • So, whether or not it's physical to start a model off with or without any energy generation coming from fusion, once you continue its evolution without fusion, you get the same result either way.

    • This exactly what I'd been hoping to find out, so it's all good news!
